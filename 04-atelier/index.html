§ VI.b — The Vetting Always two checks · No exceptions

Checked, then checked again.

Safety is the unglamorous foundation beneath everything glamorous. Every aircraft we arrange flies under audited, certified operators — vetted before they ever appear in front of a member.

We hold to an always-two-checks ethos: the operator is vetted, and the specific aircraft and crew are reviewed again for the mission at hand.

Flight crews are held to professional standards of experience and recency, and the aircraft itself is matched to the route, the weather, and the party — never the other way around.

When a flight cannot be flown to that standard, it is not flown. That is the only acceptable answer.

№ 01

Operator vetting.

Aircraft flown only under audited, certified operators — reviewed for record, maintenance discipline, and standing before they fly for V2.

№ 02

Crew standards.

Flight crews held to professional experience and recency standards, matched to the specific aircraft and route every single time.

№ 03

Aircraft review.

Every tail reviewed against the mission, the conditions, and the party. If it cannot be done to standard, it is not done at all.
